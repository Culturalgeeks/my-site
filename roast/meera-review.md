# Meera Nambiar — Target Customer Review
**Reviewer:** Meera Nambiar (fictional representative customer — composite of actual target clients)
**Profile:** Head of International Business Development, mid-sized Indian IT services company expanding into Germany and Japan. MBA, 14 years' experience. Budget authority up to ₹20L for advisory. Has been burned by generic "culture trainers" before. Discovers this site via a LinkedIn recommendation from a peer.

---

## Overall Verdict

**I would stay on this site. I am not sure I would enquire.**

Let me tell you exactly what went through my mind when I read this page.

---

## The First 10 Seconds

The first thing I saw was the headline: *"Decode. Adapt. Transcend."* followed by the story about the "wrong container." I read that paragraph twice. That line — *"intelligent, well-prepared professionals say exactly the wrong thing — not because they were wrong, but because they were speaking into the wrong container"* — described, almost precisely, a situation I lived through in a client meeting in Frankfurt eight months ago. I kept reading.

So the opening worked. You have my attention.

---

## What I Responded To

**The About section spoke to me.** The career timeline — Reliance, Saudi Arabia, ISB, China office, APAC Director — tells me this is not someone who studied culture in a classroom. This is someone who has sat in the meetings I sit in. The line *"The problem was never intelligence. It was never intent. It was always the gap between what was said and what was received across a cultural divide"* — that is exactly the language I would use to explain my problem to my board.

**The book is intriguing.** "Not a textbook. A field guide." — yes, that is what I want. The 12-country structure with a theoretical framework underneath it sounds like something I would actually use and pass on to my team leads.

**The form qualifier (the dropdown for area of interest).** I appreciated this. It told me that the person at the other end of this form is thinking about my specific need, not just collecting leads.

---

## Where I Slowed Down or Stopped

### I Could Not See Who This Is Primarily For

Halfway through the services section, I started to wonder: *am I the right client for this?* The site covers four things — leadership branding, India market entry, intercultural training, and a book. I am interested in intercultural training for my team. But the site does not tell me what format that takes, how many people, over what duration, at what investment level.

I am not asking for a price list. I am asking for enough orientation to know whether contacting you is a reasonable next step or an overreach. Even "typically delivered as a half-day or full-day workshop for leadership teams of 8–20" would answer my question.

### There Is No Photo

I realise this sounds minor, but it is not minor at the level of advisory services. I am going to be asking this person to come in front of my team — possibly my CEO and the leadership of our German partner. Before I invest political capital in that recommendation, I want to see who I am recommending. A headshot is not vanity. It is a trust signal for a services business.

### The Testimonials Do Not Help Me

"Senior Vice President, German Industrial Equipment Firm" — I cannot verify this. I cannot reach out to this person, check LinkedIn, or ask my network whether they know them. I am not accusing anyone of fabrication, but at the level of investment I am considering, I need more than an anonymous title. Even initials and a verified industry would be more credible.

I have seen training companies with fabricated testimonials. This feels similar, even if it is not.

### "Get the Book" Took Me to a Contact Form

I was genuinely interested in the book. I clicked "Get the Book" expecting either a buy link or at least a page with more detail — sample chapter, table of contents, publication date. I landed on the contact form. That felt like a bait and switch. I nearly left the page.

Where can I actually buy this book? Is it published? If not, when? I would pre-order it today if there were a way to do so.

### The CG Guide Chatbot

I opened it. The greeting is decent, but it asked me generic qualifying questions ("What brings you here today?") that I could have answered by reading the homepage. I expected it to ask something more specific — perhaps "What market are your teams most focused on?" or "Which cultural gap is costing your organisation the most right now?" The conversation did not get interesting quickly enough.

(Note: I am aware this depends on the actual backend being live. If this was tested without the API connected, the experience would be different.)

---

## What Would Make Me Enquire

1. A photo of Ehtesham — even a professional LinkedIn-style photo
2. One real testimonial with a real name (or initials) and a verifiable company
3. A clear answer to "what does the intercultural training actually look like and for whom?"
4. A real way to either buy or pre-order the book
5. A specific invite in the contact area — not "let us talk" in general, but something like: "Tell me your market, your team, and the situation you are navigating. I will respond within 48 hours with a short assessment of whether and how I can help."

That last one would push me to fill in the form immediately.

---

## Net Assessment

The writing and the credentials are strong enough that I have not left. But I have not converted either. I am in a holding pattern — curious but unconvinced. The gap between "this person knows what they are talking about" and "I trust them enough to put them in front of my organisation" has not yet been bridged.

The site is very good at making me feel like I am in good hands intellectually. It is not yet good at making me feel like I know exactly what I am buying and what happens after I click "Send."
