About the Book
About

Twenty-five years.
Five countries.
One recurring observation.

I began my career at Reliance Industries in 1998, commissioning HDD rigs in multinational environments where no two engineers communicated the same way. Over the years that followed — Saudi Arabia, an ISB MBA on the Dean's List, launching a U.S. multinational's India office, building a VR team, running the China office, and eventually overseeing Asia Pacific — the observation sharpened.

The problem was never intelligence. It was never intent. It was always the gap between what was said and what was received across a cultural divide.

When Culture Collides with Language is my attempt to give working professionals the tools I wish I had been handed at the start. The consultancy is what comes after — for those who want to apply those tools to their specific market, team, or brand.

The Book

The book I needed on day one.

When Culture Collides with Language walks working professionals through the logic — cultural values, rhetoric, politeness norms, time orientation, reader versus writer responsibility — and then applies that logic, country by country, to twelve of the world's most commercially significant cultures.

Not a textbook. A field guide.
